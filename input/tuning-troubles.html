Tuning Troubles

The Distortion Lesson

(Jonathan is onstage with his electric guitar, ready to introduce the concept. Becky looks on with concern.)

Jonathan: (self-importantly) Alright folks, we’re going to play the next song in just a minute. But first must tune my guitar. Now it’s important that I can tune my guitar without noise, distractions, or interruptions. (Looks meaningfully at Becky).

(Becky nods in an exaggerated way, signaling understanding. Jonathan starts tuning, a few seconds go by.)

Becky: Hey, Jonathan! I just have one question…

(Jonathan, absorbed in his task, raises a hand for silence without looking up.)

(Becky tries to get Jonathan’s attention a few more times, each time a bit more exaggerated. Jonathan shows increasing signs of annoyance.)

Jonathan: (sighing) Okay, okay. If I let you ask your question, will you let me tune my guitar in peace?

Becky: (nodding eagerly) Yes, yes, absolutely!

(Jonathan stops tuning and turns to face Becky, expectantly.)

Becky: (innocently) Do you know how to tune a guitar?

(Jonathan looks exasperated, placing a hand on his forehead.)

Jonathan: (sarcastically) Of course, I do! It’s what I’m trying to do RIGHT NOW!

(The audience laughs. Jonathan goes back to tuning. Becky crosses over down stage right. She goes to a bag and pulls out a rubber fish, signaling to the audience to be quiet.)

Becky: (interrupting again) Hey, Jonathan?

(Jonathan puts his hand on his face.)

Jonathan: (resigned) Yes, Becky?

Becky: (grinning) I know you can tune a guitar, but can you tuna fish?

(Before Jonathan can respond, Becky tosses the rubber fish at him. Jonathan catches it awkwardly, pretending to be flustered. The audience erupts in laughter.)
